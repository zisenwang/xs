2025 Oxbridge Foundation Camp

Camp Overview

This foundation camp is designed to help students aspiring to apply to Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial College efficiently improve their academic capabilities. Mathematics, as the logical foundation of most disciplines, is the most important subject. To ensure students can adapt to accelerated learning in various disciplines, this foundational camp is offered. All courses are delivered in English. The teaching team comprises Oxbridge graduates with high standards: over 3 years of teaching experience, successful Oxbridge student coaching experience, ability to independently design integrated lesson plans, ability to teach large classes in English, clear logic, sense of humor, and strong teaching enthusiasm and responsibility.

Introduction to the Foundation Camp

The Oxbridge Foundation Camp is specially designed for students transitioning from Year 9 to Year 10 or the second year of the two-year GCSE program. The course aims to help students adapt early to the pace of international high school curricula. It mainly focuses on applied mathematics in natural sciences. The course is taught entirely in English to familiarize students with mathematical writing and expression in English.

Target Audience

  • Students transitioning from Year 9 to Year 10, or from the first to the second year of the GCSE program
  • Students interested in STEM and aiming for G5 universities

Schedule

  • Camp Dates: 2025.7.9 - 2025.7.23
  • Morning: Systematic instruction
  • Afternoon: Practice and assessment
  • Final Assessment: Evaluating students' learning achievements

Course Content

  • Covers all essential math content required for science subjects (physics, chemistry, biology, economics)
  • Topics include: vectors, calculus, differential equations, basic statistics

Teaching Objectives

The program will be divided into three groups, each with specific objectives and content:

  • Biochemistry Group: Focuses on enhancing calculation, algebraic, statistical, and probability skills (based on ESAT)
  • Math & Economics Group: Focuses on game theory, number theory, logic, and statistics (based on TMUA)
